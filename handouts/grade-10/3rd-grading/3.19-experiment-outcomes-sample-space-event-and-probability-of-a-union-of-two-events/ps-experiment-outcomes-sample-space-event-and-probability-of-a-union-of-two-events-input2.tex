Solve the following problems.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\color{blue}\arabic*.]
    \item An experiment consists of flipping a fair coin. Let event \(A\) be getting heads, and event \(B\) be getting tails. What is the probability of getting heads or tails?
    \item In a deck of cards, let event \(A\) be drawing a red card, and event \(B\) be drawing a face card. What is the probability of drawing a red card or a face card?
    % \item A jar contains 12 marbles: 5 yellow, 4 purple, and 3 orange. Let event \(A\) be selecting a yellow marble, and event \(B\) be selecting an orange marble. What is the probability of selecting a yellow or orange marble?
    % \item A spinner has 6 sections, numbered 1 to 6. Let event \(A\) be spinning a number less than 3, and event \(B\) be spinning an odd number. What is the probability of spinning a number less than 3 or an odd number?
    \item In a survey of 50 people, 20 like coffee, 30 like tea, and 15 like both coffee and tea. What is the probability of a person liking either coffee or tea?
 %   \item A die is rolled. Let event \(A\) be rolling a number greater than 4, and event \(B\) be rolling an even number. What is the probability of rolling a number greater than 4 or an even number?
%    \item A bag contains 8 red, 6 blue, and 4 green marbles. Let event \(A\) be selecting a red marble, and event \(B\) be selecting a green marble. What is the probability of selecting either a red or green marble?
    \item A class survey showed that 40 students like swimming, 35 students like running, and 15 students like both. What is the probability that a student likes either swimming or running?
    \item A box contains 100 tickets: 25 are winning tickets, and 10 are lucky tickets. What is the probability of selecting a winning or lucky ticket?
